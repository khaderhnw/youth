S1 • Onboard to TAMM Youth / Family
S1-A • Enter Coming of Age
Cross-journey
16–17 • 18–21
Entities: FCA (life moment owner), DGE / TAMM (Youth Platform / Family Space)
  • Youth or parent opens the Coming of Age card inside TAMM Family Space.
  • Gets a clear explainer: support across study, NS, work, volunteering, money, wellbeing, entrepreneurship.
  • Vision Board and gamification are introduced as the “spine” of the journey.
S1 • Onboard
S1-B • Profile & Vision Board Setup
Cross-journey
16–17
Entities: Youth Platform (Youth 360 & Vision Board), FCA
  • Short Youth 360 wizard: education level, interests, main worries (NS, money, work, grades, family, etc.).
  • Youth adds first goals: “finish NS”, “study health”, “get first job”, “volunteer 20 hours”, “save 10,000 AED”.
  • Daily micro-question is activated and tied to the Vision Board.
S1 • Onboard
S1-C • Consents & Data Sharing
Cross-journey
16–23
Entities: ICP (identity), FCA, DGE / TAMM, ADEK, ACTVET, NSRA, Mawaheb, Volunteers.ae, Ma’an, KFED, Shory
  • One-screen consent wizard explains what data can be shared with ADEK, ACTVET, NSRA, MAWAHEB, VOLAE, Ma’an, KFED, Shory, etc.
  • Youth (and parent where relevant) toggle consents with simple language (“why we use your data”).
  • Consent flags feed dashboards so entities can see coverage and trust levels.
S2 • Discover & Explore
S2-A • Explore Education & Skills
Education & Skills
16–17 • 18–21
Entities: ADEK (ADS, Khotwa, Rize), ACTVET (TVET, EmiratesSkills / Skills Challenge), Y2W / YES2WORK
  • “Life after Grade 12” shows three rails: University (ADS, Khotwa, Rize), TVET, Y2W / YES2WORK.
  • Youth explores Abu Dhabi Scholarships (ADS), Khotwa and Rize prep programmes, plus ACTVET diplomas and skills competitions.
  • Recommendations align with Vision Board goals (e.g. health science, engineering, technical trades).
S2 • Discover
S2-B • Understand National Service
National Service
16–17 • 18–21
Entities: NSRA – National Service & Reserve Authority
  • NS explainer (obligations, duration, allowances, exemptions) with myth/fact quiz.
  • Youth indicates preferred timing: before or after study/work, stored for later NS planning.
  • NS awareness completion flag is visible to NSRA and in the Youth Record.
S2 • Discover
S2-C • Explore Work & MAWAHEB
Work & Employability
17–23
Entities: Mawaheb Talent Hub, Train for Work, Nafis, Y2W / YES2WORK, Youth Platform
  • Youth opens “Work & Experience” and sees job families and entry-level roles.
  • Creates / updates MAWAHEB profile; job preferences sync to the Vision Board.
  • Sees Train for Work opportunities and Nafis-supported roles as recommended tiles.

S2-D • Volunteering & Civic Roles
Volunteering & Civic
16–23
Entities: VOLAE / Volunteers.ae, Takatof, Sanid, AD Police (We Are All Police), ADCD
  • Youth browses volunteering opportunities by cause (environment, sports, events, safety, etc.).

S2 • Discover
S2-E • Money, Ghaya & Shory (Insurance)
Money & Insurance
16–23
Entities: Ma’an (Ghaya financial literacy), Shory Insurance
  • Youth opens “Money & Protection” and sketches a first saving goal (e.g. emergency fund, car, study gap).
  • Sees Ghaya financial literacy programme as the structured path to improve money decisions.
  • Shory content explains car/vehicle and other personal insurance basics in youth-friendly language, with links to compare options.
S2 • Discover
S2-F • Wellbeing & Entrepreneurship
Wellbeing & Entrepreneurship
16–23
Entities: Ma’an (wellbeing & Social Incubator), Health sector / DOH, KFED – Khalifa Fund
  • Wellbeing topics (stress, sleep, exam anxiety, NS anxiety, family tension) with self-check tools and support options.
  • Entrepreneurship intro: local founder stories, Ma’an Social Incubator and KFED funding/training.
  • Daily mood slider; sustained high stress surfaces Ma’an / health support journeys.
S3 • Decide & Plan
S3-A • Choose Path Scenario
Cross-journey
17–21
Entities: ADEK, ACTVET, NSRA, Mawaheb, Train for Work, Nafis
  • “Plan my path” wizard offers scenarios: Study-first, NS-first, Work-first, mixed (study + work, etc.).
  • Shows impact on timing of scholarships (ADS / Khotwa / Rize), TVET, NS, Train for Work and Nafis opportunities.
  • Selected scenario is pinned visually on the Vision Board timeline.
S3 • Decide & Plan
S3-B • Link Goals to Programmes
Cross-journey
18–23
Entities: ADEK, ACTVET, NSRA, Mawaheb, Train for Work, Nafis, VOLAE / Takatof / Sanid, AD Police, ADCD, Ma’an / Ghaya / MSI, Shory, KFED
  • For each Vision Board goal, youth chooses 1–3 concrete programmes (ADS / Khotwa / Rize, TVET, NS track, Train for Work, Nafis jobs, volunteering, Ghaya, Shory, KFED / MSI, etc.).
  • Goal–programme matrix highlights goals with no attached actions for follow-up.
  • Same matrix powers entity dashboards to see alignment between goals and their initiatives.
S3 • Decide & Plan
S3-C • Money Plan (Ghaya-linked)
Money & Insurance
18–23
Entities: Ma’an – Ghaya financial literacy
  • Youth sets a saving target (amount & deadline) linked to Ghaya (e.g. “save 15k AED in 18 months”).
  • Sees a simple monthly saving breakdown and suggested Ghaya modules.
  • Money goal is locked on the Vision Board and tracked against Ghaya completion and self-reported behaviour.
S3 • Decide & Plan
S3-D • Volunteer & Entrepreneurship Planning
Volunteering & Entrepreneurship
18–23
Entities: VOLAE / Takatof / Sanid, AD Police, ADCD, KFED, Ma’an Social Incubator
  • Youth sets an annual volunteering hours target and picks causes to focus on.
  • Completes an entrepreneurship readiness check; results suggest KFED programmes or Ma’an Social Incubator steps.
  • Both targets (hours and venture readiness) appear on the Vision Board and in entity dashboards.
S4 • Apply
S4-A • Multi-rail Applications
Cross-journey
18–23
Entities: ADEK (ADS / Khotwa / Rize), ACTVET / EmiratesSkills / Y2W, NSRA, Mawaheb, Train for Work, Nafis, VOLAE / Takatof / Sanid, Ma’an / Ghaya / MSI, Shory, KFED, FCA, DGE / TAMM
  • Single TAMM-style apply shell reuses Youth 360 data across scholarships, TVET, NS, MAWAHEB jobs, Train for Work, Nafis, volunteering, Ghaya, Shory and KFED / MSI forms.

S5 • Participate & Track
S5-A • One View of Progress
Cross-journey
18–23
Entities: FCA, DGE / TAMM, ADEK, ACTVET / EmiratesSkills / Y2W, NSRA, Mawaheb, Train for Work, Nafis, VOLAE / Takatof / Sanid, Ma’an / Ghaya / MSI, Shory, KFED, Health sector, AD Police, ADCD

S6 • Transition & Next Steps
S6-A • After School / NS / Graduation
Cross-journey
19–23
Entities: NSRA, Mawaheb, Nafis, Ma’an (support), Health sector, FCA, DGE / Youth Platform
  • Automatic triggers when youth completes Grade 12, NS or university.
  • Transition wizard offers tailored next steps: job search (Mawaheb / Nafis / Train for Work), further study, entrepreneurship (KFED / MSI), or support if stuck (Ma’an, health services).
  • Youth can generate a “Youth Record” (education, NS, work, volunteering, Ghaya, skills, XP) to share with employers or programmes.
S7 • Ongoing Support & Engagement
S7-A • Daily Loop, Rewards & Community
Cross-journey
18–23
Entities: Youth Platform / TAMM, FCA, DGE, all partners via campaigns & communities
  • On every TAMM open, youth sees one context-aware micro-task (NS next step, application nudge, volunteering reminder, Ghaya tip, wellbeing check, venture task, etc.).
  • XP tiers and rewards catalogue recognise real actions; alumni and interest-based communities connect youth across programmes.
  • Parent / family view summarises progress and upcoming milestones without exposing sensitive details.

Education & Skills
18–23

S5 • Participate & Track
J1 • First Job – HS & National Service
Work & Employability
19–23
Entities: NSRA, Mawaheb Talent Hub, Train for Work, Nafis, Youth Platform
  • NS levels and HS streams map to job families; MAWAHEB shows matching roles in government and private sectors.
