Education & Skills
Low awareness Eligibility confusion Unified catalogue NS coordination
What it does
  • Abu Dhabi Scholarships (ADS), Khotwa and Rize study support & bridging programmes.
  • Fund and prepare youth for local & international higher education.
Key challenges today
  • Youth discover ADS/Khotwa/Rize unevenly via schools, social media, word of mouth.
  • Eligibility and test/document requirements can be unclear and spread across sites.
TO‑BE impact
  • Scholarships surfaced in Coming of Age as personalised “for you” cards with eligibility badges.
  • Unified apply shell and NS timing coordination via Vision Board path planning.

Academic bias Fragmented info Rail next to university
What it does
  • Provides vocational diplomas and applied training as alternatives to purely academic routes.
  • Linked to Y2W / YES2WORK for smoother school‑to‑work transitions.
Key challenges today
  • TVET often perceived as “second choice”; information scattered across separate channels.

  • ACTVET appears alongside university as an equal rail in “Life after Grade 12”.
  • Programme status and progression visible in Youth Record and “first job” flows.

NSRA – National Service & Reserve
NSRA – National Service
S2 Discover S3 Decide S4 Apply S5 Participate S6 Transition
National Service
Anxiety & myths Timing with study/work One NS status view
What it does
  • Manages call‑up, medical, service and completion of National Service for eligible youth.
  • Runs NS awareness events, NS Career Fair, Hama campaigns.
Key challenges today
  • Information often arrives late or via SMS; service timing not integrated with education / job planning.
  • Registration and status checks are separate from other youth services.
TO‑BE impact
  • NS appears as one rail inside Coming of Age with awareness modules, timing preference and unified status.
  • NS completion feeds Youth Record and triggers dedicated “after NS” transitions (job, study, entrepreneurship).
